Part I

Core Concepts

What is Vibe Coding

A new programming paradigm: instead of writing code line by line, you describe your intent in natural language and let an AI agent generate, debug, and iterate on the entire system. Your role shifts from "coder" to "director" — setting direction, reviewing output, and making critical decisions.

This does not lower the bar — quite the opposite: it demands even higher standards in precision of requirement expression, aesthetic judgment, and systems thinking.

SSpec (Specification)

The "blueprint" for the AI: objectives, functional requirements, constraints, visual and interaction intent, acceptance criteria. The quality of the Spec directly determines the quality of the output.

Analogy: A Spec is to Vibe Coding what an experimental protocol is to research — write it well, and you can reproduce, iterate, and communicate.

PPlan Mode

Claude Code's read-only analysis mode — the AI only explores code, analyzes structure, and proposes solutions without making any changes. Activate via Shift+Tab or claude --permission-mode plan.

Use Plan Mode first to align on an approach, then move to implementation. Think before you build.

AAgentic Loop

The core workflow of Claude Code: analyze → act → verify → correct, iterating automatically until complete. You can interrupt and redirect at any time.

  • 1. Delegate, don't dictate
  • 2. Plan first, build second
  • 3. Continuously calibrate
  • 4. Aesthetics are functional
  • 5. Iterate the Spec
  • 6. Leverage the ecosystem

Part IV

Comprehensive Assessment: Research Field Navigator

Overview

Build a Research Field Navigator: input your own doctoral research field, and the system automatically completes the full research intelligence pipeline — from data collection, citation graph, trend analysis, and controversy debate to research proposals.

Composed of 7 modules, you must complete all of them, but you can vary the depth of each — we recommend choosing 2-3 modules to polish deeply while keeping the rest functionally complete.

Key constraint: You must use your own doctoral research field as input. All module outputs must revolve around your actual research direction.
1 Data Collection→ 2 Citation Graph→ 3 Trend Analysis→ 4 Research Proposals→ 5 Controversy Analysis→ 6 Integrated Interface→ 7 Reflection
1 Data Collection Pipeline 10%

Build an end-to-end paper data collection pipeline.

  • Fetch paper metadata from at least two different public data sources covering the past 1-2 years of your research field
  • Handle pagination, rate limiting, and format differences across data sources
  • Clean dirty data, deduplicate across sources, and store in a unified persistent format
  • Support incremental updates (do not re-fetch existing data)
  • Collect no fewer than 200 papers in total

2 Citation Graph Visualization 15%

Build an interactive citation graph based on the collected data.

  • Starting from seed papers, recursively expand citation relationships to 2 levels deep without crashing at 200+ nodes
  • Automatically detect key nodes (highly-cited hubs, cross-subfield bridges)
  • Use visual encoding to convey information hierarchy — intuitively readable without a legend
  • Interactive exploration: click for details and AI summaries, filter by time/topic/impact
Visualization quality is the core evaluation criterion for this module — this is a key test of aesthetic ability.

4 Research Gaps & Proposals 15%

Systematically discover research gaps and generate proposals based on data from the first three modules.

  • Identify gaps through topic modeling, citation network analysis, method-problem cross-referencing, etc. — every gap must be supported by data evidence
  • Generate structured research proposals for each gap, at least 3
  • Design an explainable novelty scoring framework for self-evaluation
  • Link each proposal to supporting papers, forming a traceable evidence chain
Each proposal should be refined to one page or less, reading like a formal research proposal.

5 Controversy Multi-Perspective Analysis 15%

Select a controversial topic in your research field and conduct an in-depth multi-perspective analysis.

  • Decompose into debatable sub-issues
  • At least 3 differentiated AI personas engaging in multi-round exchanges grounded in real literature
  • Synthesis report: with argumentative rhythm, distinction between strong and weak evidence, and labeled consensus vs. disagreement
  • Each key claim annotated with evidence source and strength (strong empirical / indirect inference / theoretical conjecture)
The report should read like a high-quality scientific commentary, not an AI bullet-point list.

6 Integrated Interface & Report 20%

Integrate all modules into a unified interactive interface and comprehensive report.

7 Reflection & Spec Iteration 15%

A retrospective on the entire build process — this is not a code module, but an assessment of metacognitive ability.

  • Submit the full iteration history of your Spec (at least 2 versions), showing the rationale behind each revision
  • Write a reflection report (800 words or fewer), addressing:
    1. How did the Spec evolve, and what triggered each revision?
    2. In which aspects of each module did the AI exceed expectations?
    3. Which aspects required manual intervention, and why?
    4. If you could redo the project, what would you change?
The quality of expression in the reflection report is itself an evaluation criterion — precise, restrained, and insightful.

Part V

Assessment Guidelines

Time Allocation

Total duration: 6-8 hours. We recommend spending the first 30 minutes reading through all modules to understand the overall architecture before starting with Module 1.

Depth Distribution

Choose 2-3 modules to polish deeply, while keeping the rest functionally complete. Scoring considers both overall completion and depth of focus modules.

Deliverables

Spec document (with iteration versions), code repository (runnable), screenshots/screen recordings, design rationale (200-300 words), reflection report (800 words or fewer).

Scoring Process

  1. Review Output First — Launch the system, walk through the full flow, record first impressions
  2. Module-by-Module Evaluation — Deep-dive into each module, note quality
  3. Review Spec & Rationale — Assess the quality of "intent → output" translation
  4. Read Reflection Last — Evaluate depth of thinking and expression quality
  5. Comparative Scoring — Cross-compare candidates in the same cohort to avoid anchoring bias
